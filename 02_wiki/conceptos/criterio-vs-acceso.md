# Criterio vs acceso

## Definición

The first wave of AI-assisted knowledge systems solved discovery and access. The next wave has to solve criterion: what is supported, what is only plausible, and what should remain open.

## Evidencia firme

- Andrews argues that when execution becomes cheaper, judgement becomes more valuable.
- Azaustre demonstrates that automation can maintain a wiki, but does not by itself distinguish stronger from weaker epistemic claims.
- Together, they suggest that the difficult layer is no longer access alone but governed interpretation.

## Lectura abierta

This shift matters especially for humanities-oriented work, where the problem is not only finding passages but deciding how far an interpretation can be carried without disguising it as evidence.

## Hipótesis abierta

- A future public category may emerge around `editorial knowledge bases`, where AI systems are judged less by recall and more by how they expose uncertainty and support.
