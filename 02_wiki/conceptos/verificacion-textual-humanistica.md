# Verificación textual humanística

## Definición

The problem of confirming that a quotation, formula, or attributed claim actually appears in the source it claims to come from — in the edition it claims to come from, at the location it claims to come from. In humanities, this is harder than substring matching because texts circulate in translations, paraphrases, and apocryphal reformulations.

## Evidencia firme

- Lacan's seminars are a canonical case: formulas are routinely attributed to the wrong seminar, quoted from secondary sources, or fabricated outright. Ateneo has caught phantom citations in published academic material.
- Standard search tools (Ctrl+F, full-text search) fail when the problem is not finding a string but confirming that a specific formulation belongs to a specific source.

## Lectura abierta

The gap between what LLMs retrieve and what humanists need to verify may define a durable research category — one where AI-assisted citation checking requires domain-specific editorial infrastructure, not just better retrieval.

## Hipótesis abierta

- If verification becomes a visible layer in AI-assisted research tools, the distinction between "found" and "confirmed" may reshape how scholars interact with large textual corpora.
